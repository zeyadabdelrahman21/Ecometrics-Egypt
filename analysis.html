Targeting Strategic Investments

This dashboard sheet prioritizes commercial viability, focusing on where collection infrastructure and dry recyclable volumes overlap to maximize potential recycling ROI.

Key Investment Insights:

  • Dry Recyclable Volume: Cairo and Giza generate over 500k tons of waste combined monthly, making them the priority locations for modern material recovery facilities (MRFs).
  • Infrastructure Gap: Delta and Upper Egypt regions have huge generation volumes but suffer from low collection coverage (~36%). Investing in municipal collection networks here provides the largest social and environmental returns.

Bivariate & Statistical Modeling

The final dashboard sheet investigates causal relationships. Our star schema model links economic development, municipal infrastructure, and recycling performance.

Key Research Discoveries:

  • The Collection Trap: Regions with low collection coverage face depressed formal recycling rates because raw materials never enter formal waste processing facilities.
  • Income Effects: Areas with higher GDP per capita yield significantly higher plastic and packaging fractions, shifting processing requirements from composting toward material sorting and recovery.
